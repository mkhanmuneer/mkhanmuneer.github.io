Executive Summary

In professional analytics, the value of a system is defined by its autonomy and its architecture. This project documents the engineering of a High-Performance Market Valuation Engine that transforms 2,930 raw housing records into a live, automated intelligence ecosystem.

By pivoting from a redundant flat-file to a sophisticated Star Schema and implementing an automated OAuth2-based refresh pipeline, I achieved an 82.5% reduction in memory footprint while increasing analytical depth by 79%. This engine doesn’t just describe the market; it provides a scalable blueprint for enterprise-grade operational intelligence.

The Engineering Ledger: From “Dark Data” to Automation

Technical ROI & Model Evolution

The initial diagnostic of the raw data revealed a classic “Dark Data” bottleneck: 100% of columns were non-performant strings. The transition to a production-ready engine required a fundamental architectural shift.

Metric Raw Dataset (df_raw) Valuation Engine (df_fact) Improvement
Memory Footprint 13.73 MB 2.40 MB 82.5% Reduction
Data Types 100% Strings/Objects 95% Numeric/Keys Engine Optimized
Feature Richness 82 Variables 147 Variables +79% Intelligence
Schema Type Flat-File (Redundant) Star Schema (Normalized) Production Ready

Production-Grade Automation

To move beyond static reporting, I engineered an automated update pipeline using the Power BI REST API. - Authentication: Implemented an OAuth2 flow using a Microsoft Azure Service Principal (Tenant, Client, and Secret ID integration). - Orchestration: The pipeline automates the POST request to the Power BI /refreshes endpoint, ensuring the Star Schema is updated and appended with new market data without manual intervention. - Result: A self-sustaining intelligence loop that guarantees “Time-to-Insight” is minimized.

Market Valuation Dashboard: Narrative Analysis

Page 1: Market Executive Overview

Visualizing the Pulse of the City

The entry point of the engine focuses on strategic drivers. By engineering Neighborhood Tiers (Premium, Mid-Market, and Value), the dashboard allows for “Apple-to-Apple” comparisons. The “Price vs. Size & Quality” bubble chart validates my Quality-Adjusted Living Area feature, proving that in a mature market, quality ratings often command a higher premium than raw square footage.

Page 2: Neighborhood & Property Profiling

Granular Market Segmentation

This page leverages the House Age Category logic (e.g., Mature, Modern, Established). Given that the market under discussion is dominated by properties older than 50 years, this segmentation allows stakeholders to isolate the “Years Since Remodel” impact, identifying which older neighborhoods are seeing the highest capital appreciation through modern updates.

Page 3: Advanced Analytical Insights

Predictive Diagnostics & Temporal Trends

The final page demonstrates analytical maturity through YoY/MoM Sales Change and Statistical Distribution (Box Plots). By segmenting price by both Neighborhood and Quality, the dashboard allows investors to identify “Value Tiers”—areas where high-quality homes are currently undervalued relative to the market mean.

Strategic Roadmap (v2.0)

This engine is currently undergoing continuous deployment. The following features are in the engineering and testing phase to further reduce “time-to-insight” for professional users:

  • Market Quadrant Analysis: Deployment of Treemaps and Bar Charts to visualize the four-dimensional relationship between Price and Area (e.g., High Price / Low Area “Urban Luxury” vs. Low Price / High Area “Value Scale”).
  • Tier Comparison View: An interactive toggle allowing users to benchmark property performance against specific Neighborhood Tiers (Premium, Mid-Market, Value) rather than generic city-wide medians.
  • Valuation Variance Logic: Automated alerts when a property’s Price-per-SF deviates significantly from its Tier-specific baseline.

Data Architecture Summary

The engine follows a strict Star Schema design to maximize the efficiency of the Power BI VeriPaq columnar storage engine: - Fact_Sales: Optimized numeric fact table with integer-based keys. - Dim_Neighborhood: Strategic tiers and geographic attributes. - Dim_Property_Details: Normalized building styles and quality metrics. - Dim_Date: Automated date keys for YoY and MoM growth tracking. —
